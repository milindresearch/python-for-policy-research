# Day 04 - Scatter Plots

## What is a Scatter Plot?

A scatter plot shows the relationship between two numerical variables.

Each point represents one observation.

---

## Core Command

```python
plt.scatter(x, y)
```

---

## Why Analysts Use It

Scatter plots help identify:

- Positive relationships
- Negative relationships
- Outliers
- Trends
- Correlations

---

## Policy Examples

GDP Growth vs Defence Spending

GDP Per Capita vs Military Expenditure

Inflation vs Interest Rates

Education Spending vs Literacy Rate

Trade Volume vs Economic Growth

---

## Example Interpretation

If GDP growth increases and defence spending also increases, the points move upward from left to right.

This suggests a positive relationship.

---

## Key Takeaway

Scatter plots are one of the most important tools in econometrics because they help analysts visually inspect relationships before running statistical models.
