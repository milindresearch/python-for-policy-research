# Day 03 - Histograms

## What is a Histogram?

A histogram shows the distribution of numerical data.

Unlike bar charts, histograms group values into ranges called bins.

---

## Why It Matters

Analysts use histograms to understand:

- Distribution
- Concentration
- Skewness
- Outliers
- Data quality

---

## Core Command

```python
plt.hist(data)
```

Creates a histogram.

---

## Policy Applications

- Defence spending distribution
- Income distribution
- Inflation observations
- GDP growth rates
- Trade volumes

---

## Example Question

Are most countries spending between 50 and 100 billion USD on defence?

A histogram helps answer this immediately.

---

## Key Takeaway

Histograms help analysts understand how data is distributed before conducting statistical or econometric analysis.
